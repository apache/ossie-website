## Overview

The Open Semantic Interchange (OSI) specification defines a vendor-neutral,
human-readable format for describing semantic models — the curated layer of
metrics, dimensions, relationships, and business logic that sits between raw
data and end users.

The specification is designed so that a semantic model can be defined once and
exchanged freely between analytics, AI, and business intelligence platforms.

## Core Concepts

The specification is organized around these top-level constructs:

- **Data Sources** — connections to databases, warehouses, and lakes
- **Entities** — logical objects (tables, views, or virtual datasets)
- **Relationships** — joins and associations between entities
- **Metrics & Measures** — quantitative calculations with well-defined semantics
- **Dimensions & Hierarchies** — categorical attributes and drill paths
- **Access Policies** — row-level and column-level security definitions
